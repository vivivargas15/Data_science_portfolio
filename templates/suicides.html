Project 1: Exploring suicide data (EDA) with PYTHON.

Prepared by: Viviana Vargas

In this article I want to carry out an exploratory analysis of (crude) suicide rates in the world. The database is published by the WHO and includes records from 2000 to 2019 by country. The main objective is to detect which continents and countries have the highest and lowest suicide rates.

The entire project can be consulted in its GitHub repository.

1. Information download

The database can be downloaded free of charge from the WHO website.
For reading, cleaning and processing we will use pandas, matplotlib, seaborn, numpy and plotly.

2. Data processing

The .head() method gives us a sample of the database, highlighting that columns with NaN values can now be displayed.


We create a new data frame with the variables of interest
Description of variables

  • ParentLocation= continent
  • Location = country
  • Period = year
  • Dim1 = sex
  • FactValueNumeric = suicide rate per 100,000 inhabitants.
Subsequent analyzes consider only the Both sexes category of the Dim1 variable, to facilitate comparisons between countries.


We only analyzed the category “Both sexes” => df_analisis


We sort df_analisis by FactValueNumeric to obtain the top ten of the highest suicide rates in (2000-2019)


Unfortunately, only one country "Lesotho" appears in this top ten of the highest suicide rates.
Lesotho is a small country in Africa, mountainous, landlocked, its population lives in rural areas and is dedicated to subsistence agriculture. It has more than 2,000,000 inhabitants.

Analysis for the year 2019

Countries with the highest suicide rate in 2019
Again Lesotho is in first place, however, 6 of the 10 countries are from the African continent (Lesotho, Eswatini, Zimbabwe, South Africa, Mozambique and Central African Republic), 2 from America (Guyana and Suriname) and 2 from the Western Pacific (Kiribati and Micronesia)
